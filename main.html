The following list is time line of Henry Ford

  • 1863 - Henry was born in Michigan in 1863.

  • 1879 - Henry moved away from his family's farm to begin work in a factory in Detroit. He eventually went to work for Edison Illuminating Company.

  • 1888 - Henry married a girl from his hometown of Greenfield Township. They lived in Detroit, Michigan.

  • 1896 - Henry's first automobile was called the Quadricycle because it had four wheels. He rode it through the streets of Detroit.

  • 1899 - Henry quit his position with the Edison Illuminating Company to work with Detroit Automobile Company. He soon became chief engineer.

  • 1901 - Ford opened his first car company in 1901, but he quickly resigned when there was a dispute among the bankers. In 1902 it became the Cadillac Motor Car Co.

  • 1903 - Henry Ford opened a second motor car company, calling it the Ford Motor Company. The first car produced was the Model A.

  • 1908 - In 1908 Henry Ford began producing the Model T. It quickly became famous.

  • 1913 - Ford set up the first automobile assembly line, and the cars were made faster and cheaper than ever.

  • 1918 - Henry Ford set out for the U.S. Senate, but he was turned down.

  • 1921 - By 1921, Ford Motor Company was putting out more than 55% of the cars in the country.

  • 1926 - Ford decided to focus his efforts on more than just cars, and he ended up with an airplane called the Tri-Motor plane.

  • 1932 - Henry Ford successfully put out his first V-8 engine. It was the V-8 Ford.

  • 1941 - In spite of his earlier attempts to keep the company out of the union, Ford Motor Company joined UAW in 1941.

  • 1947 - Henry died in his home at the age of 83. He was a dedicated, hard worker who used his ideas to change America.
